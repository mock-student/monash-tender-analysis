2. Employer Registration & Onboarding

2.1 Registration Flow — CareerAxis Migration

Employers migrated from the legacy CareerAxis platform are onboarded via a dedicated migration flow. This is the primary onboarding path for existing employer accounts at go-live.

Trigger: The system sends an onboarding email to the contact who was first registered in CareerAxis for each migrated company. The email contains a link to complete their profile setup on CSM.

Profile setup flow:

  1. Contact clicks through and sets a new CSM password.
  1. They are presented with their migrated company information, pre-filled from CareerAxis data. Fields displayed for review: company name, primary country of operation, location, contact details, and address.
  1. They are prompted to upload their company logo.
  1. Any required fields that could not be migrated are flagged and must be completed before proceeding. The contact cannot advance until all required fields are filled.
  1. Once all required fields are filled, the contact clicks "Let's Begin" to complete profile setup and become the Employer Admin for their company.

Migrated job postings: Existing job postings from CareerAxis are migrated as a separate flow after company onboarding is complete. Migrated postings are not automatically published.

2.2 Registration Flow — Self-Registration

Employers register on the CSM platform without SSO — each employer creates their own username and password. The registration form opens with a company search field. Two paths follow.

Path A — Company already exists (join request):

Employer selects their company from results and fills in: name, email, phone/office number, job title (free text), and designation type (Section 2.5). A join request is routed to the assigned IRC for Staff-level review. On approval, contact is added as base Employer. On rejection, contact is notified by email with a reason.

Path B — Company does not exist (new company registration):

Employer selects "Singapore-registered company" or "Overseas-registered company," then proceeds through a four-step wizard: (1) UEN & Verify (Singapore) or Company Details (Overseas), (2) Hiring Details, (3) Primary Contact, (4) Review.

  • Singapore companies: entering a UEN triggers a real-time ACRA lookup that pre-fills company name, industry, company size, business address, and website — all editable before continuing. If the UEN returns no match or the ACRA call fails, a "UEN verification failed" modal blocks progression; clicking "Go Back" returns to the UEN entry step, with no limit on retries.
  • Overseas companies: all company details are entered manually. Company ID is not entered by the employer — it is auto-generated only once CAO approves.

Form collects: UEN (or overseas company ID, auto-generated on approval), industry (NTU classification — Section 2.6), the four required routing fields (below), primary contact details, and an optional free-text field for additional information.

No account credentials are collected during this flow — the employer sets up their password only after CAO approval, via the link sent at that stage.

Source: Linear SU-4474 · Original Requirements 6.1.1.35.1, 6.1.1.35.2, 6.1.1.40.1, 6.2.1.7.1, 6.2.1.7.2

Required routing fields (each with a tooltip definition shown to the employer):

  • Primary country of operation — the country where the company is legally registered and primarily operates
  • HR team location — the country or region where the HR/hiring team managing this account is based
  • Primary hiring market — whether the company is primarily hiring for Singapore-based roles or primarily overseas-based roles (single-select: Singapore / Overseas)
  • Countries hiring for — specific country or countries the company intends to hire for

Approval routing is determined by the combination of HR team location and primary hiring market (see Section 2.4).

On approval by Manager or Admin: company is created in CSM, registering contact becomes Employer Admin, and company is assigned an IRC based on industry (Section 2.4).

2.3 Registration Flow — CAO-Invited Employer

CAO can invite an employer directly from the Users module (Employers tab).

  1. Admin or Manager searches for the company by name or UEN — soft warning shown if a similar company exists (not a hard block).
  1. If the company does not exist, Admin or Manager creates it.
  1. Any Staff member or above adds a primary contact (name and email).
  1. System sends an invitation email to the primary contact.
  1. Primary contact clicks through to a new password-set screen (unique design — not reusing student/staff onboarding patterns).
  1. After setting their password, contact is directed to complete the company profile (Section 3).

Individual contact invites: CAO may also invite an individual contact (e.g. a company executive met at a networking event) without first creating a company entity. In this case, CAO creates the individual contact record and associates it with an existing company if applicable. If no company exists, a new company entity must be created first. The contact sets their password and is directed to complete their profile.

2.6 Industry Classification

Each company is assigned one industry from the NTU custom industry list (single-select, one-to-one). CAO Admins and Managers can create, edit, and manage this list (same pattern as the labels flow). The list can be uploaded via file to support other institutions. The ACRA industry field (from the government API on UEN lookup) is stored as a read-only field on the company record — it cannot be edited and is not linked to the NTU industry classification.

2.8 CAO Review Queue — Company Registrations

CAO Admins review incoming new company registrations (Section 2.2, Path B) in a dedicated Company Registrations queue, separate from the individual join-request queue (Path A).

Queue: Lists every incoming request with company name, industry, assigned IRC/GE Manager, contact person, email, date registered, and days elapsed. Filterable by any column.

Review panel: Selecting a request opens a panel showing the submitted company details, contact details, and uploaded documents (business registration certificate, company profile, tax clearance certificate), each viewable and downloadable by the admin.

Status timeline: Each request shows a timeline of Application Received → Admin Review in Progress, followed by one of: More Information Requested → Information Provided (if the admin requests additional detail and the employer responds), or a final Approved/Rejected outcome.

Admin actions:

  • Approve — triggers automatic IRC/GE Manager assignment by industry (Section 2.4 routing logic); the admin can override the auto-assigned IRC/GE Manager before confirming. Approval grants the employer account access to post job/internship listings, publish their public profile, and appear in the employer directory.
  • Reject — requires a reason, which is included in the rejection email sent to the applicant.
  • Request More Information — requires a message, which is emailed to the applicant and logged in the timeline; the applicant's response is likewise logged as an "Information Provided" entry.
  • Change IRC — lets the admin reassign the primary IR consultant on an already-approved employer; the newly assigned IRC is notified by email and becomes the employer's primary point of contact.

3. Company Profile

3.1 Profile Setup

After setting their password, an employer contact must complete 2FA setup (AWS Cognito TOTP) before proceeding to their company profile. Required fields: company name (pre-filled), NTU industry classification, primary contact details. The NTU industry classification list is the same Admin-managed custom list described in Section 2.6. Optional fields: about section (rich text), social media links, additional office addresses (Employer Admin only — for multi-site companies; design pending, see SU-4752).

Key Contacts require at least one contact with exactly one marked as primary. A company may have more than one Employer Admin account, with a minimum of one enforced at all times. Adding additional team members during onboarding is optional and can be skipped.

3.2 Company Portal Homepage

Logged-in employers land on their company profile page. The page shows: about section, social media links, key contacts, active job listings block, and upcoming events block. The Employer Admin sidebar includes a "Contacts" navigation item; the Employer sidebar does not. For the Employer role, the Open Opportunities block includes a toggle between own listings and all company listings.

A "View as Student" preview mode lets employers see how their profile appears to students, using live data. All edit affordances are hidden in preview mode, and Desktop/Mobile view toggles are available.

4. Contact Management

4.1 Contact Records

Each contact record stores: name, email, phone/office number, job title (free text), designation type, Email Subscribers only flag, and account status (Active/Deactivated). Contacts are filterable by designation type and industry in the CAO employer database view.

4.2 Email Subscribers Flag

Contacts marked Email Subscribers only receive email communications only and are excluded from in-platform notifications. Settable by CAO Staff and above, and by Employer Admin for their own company's contacts. Executive designation type contacts default to this setting.

Linear: SU-4489

4.3 Auto-Deactivation

Contacts inactive beyond a configurable threshold (X days/months, set by Admin) are automatically deactivated, with a warning email sent beforehand. Hard-bounce emails also trigger auto-deactivation. Deactivated contacts do not receive system communications and can be manually reactivated by CAO Admin or Manager.

Linear: SU-4485

4.4 Importing Non-Account Contacts

Authorised users can add employer contacts who have no platform account — either manually one at a time or in bulk via CSV — for inclusion in mass email campaigns via the Communications module. Non-account contacts receive no platform login access and are available only in the Communications recipient picker.

Manual entry: The contact form collects first name, last name, email (required), company, and notes. The company field does not require an existing platform record — new company names are accepted. For Singapore-registered companies, the field triggers a UEN lookup against ACRA and surfaces matching suggestions; if no match is found, the user can still proceed.

Bulk CSV upload: Follows the same all-or-nothing rule as SGCC bulk upload. If any row contains an error, the entire file is rejected and nothing is saved. On rejection, the user receives a downloadable error report — the original CSV with an "Error" column appended as the last column. The user corrects and re-uploads.

Access to this flow is controlled by RBAC permissions configured at the workspace level. Role restrictions must not be hardcoded.

5.3 Posting a New Job

Employers can create a new job listing via three methods.

Method 1 — Bulk upload (PDF or Word): Upload up to 20 PDF or .docx files at once via drag-and-drop. Each file corresponds to one job listing. The system parses each document and pre-fills available fields; any field the parser could not populate shows a validation error. The employer reviews all uploaded jobs in a batch "Review & Edit Jobs" screen — a sidebar lists every job (title, source filename, status) with a running completed/needs-review count. "Submit All for Publication" stays disabled until every job is complete. "Continue Later" exits the batch without submitting; unfinished jobs remain as individual Draft rows in the main Jobs table, each showing a "Created via PDF, needs review" banner when reopened. Employer sets Publish Date and Expiry per job before it counts as complete. Word (.docx) upload is in scope but not yet reflected in the current Figma design (PDF only) — see Open Items.

Method 2 — Manual entry: Employer fills in all job fields directly via a form. The fields shown adapt to the Job Type(s) selected — Duration appears only when an Internship type is checked, Start Date appears only when Full-time/Contract is checked, and Location switches to full address fields when Full-time is combined with an Internship type. Preferred Schools & Programmes are AI-suggested with Accept All/Reject All controls. Employer sets Publish Date and Expiry at submission, then submits for CAO approval.

Method 3 — Duplicate from previous listing: Employer browses and selects up to 20 listings posted within the past 12 months, then reviews and edits each duplicated job individually (with autosave) before batch-submitting. Only job-content fields are copied from the original — Publish Date and Expiry must be set fresh per job. On the CAO admin side, duplicated listings are tagged as reposts; a "no changes detected" indicator for unedited duplicates is planned but not yet designed (see Open Items).

Submission and approval flow (all methods):

  1. Employer submits → confirmation email sent (message: log in to view status).
  1. CAO Staff (assigned IRC) reviews and may take one of three actions:
  • Approve → listing is saved and employer is notified.
  • Reject → CAO-typed rejection reason is visible to employer on login; listing is closed.
  • Request changes → IRC proposes specific field-level edits and returns the listing to the employer. Employer is notified by email.
  1. On Request changes: the employer is shown a diff view comparing their original submission against the IRC's proposed changes. The employer may accept the proposed changes, modify them further, or revert to their original values, then resubmit.
  1. On resubmission after requested changes: the IRC is shown a three-way comparison — the original employer submission, the IRC's proposed changes, and the employer's actual resubmission — to facilitate a clear decision.
  1. On approval → listing saved as Draft; employer must actively Publish.
  1. Employer chooses Publish Now (immediately live) or Publish Later (scheduled date/time).
Steps 3–4 (Draft → employer-chosen Publish Now/Publish Later) describe the original spec and no longer apply to any of the three creation methods. All three (Bulk upload, Manual entry, Duplicate) now capture Publish Date and Expiry upfront at submission, before CAO review — the system auto-publishes at the stored Publish Date once approved, immediately if that date has already passed or on schedule if still in the future. See Conflicts Flagged for the correction history.

5.5 Student Hiring & Placement Confirmation

When an employer sets a student's application status to Hired, the following workflow is triggered:

  1. Student notification: The student is notified by the system that they have been selected for the position.
  1. Document submission: The student is required to submit relevant documents for placement confirmation. These are routed to their school/faculty and to CAO for review and approval.
  1. Placement confirmation: Once all parties have confirmed the placement, the system auto-generates a placement letter for the student.
  1. Faculty Supervisor assignment: A faculty supervisor from the student's school is assigned to oversee the placement. This assignment is made by CAO within CSM. The faculty supervisor has restricted access to CSM — they can view only their own supervisees' internship details (company, duration) and assessments submitted by the employer supervisor, and can submit their own independent assessment of the student. They cannot access any other part of the platform. A dedicated RBAC structure for this role is pending and will be defined in Phase 4.
  1. Employer Supervisor assignment: The employer is prompted to assign an Internship Supervisor from their company. The employer uploads the supervisor's contact details (name, email, phone/office number). CAO and the faculty supervisor can view the assigned employer supervisor's details from the placement record.
⚠️ Note: This workflow applies primarily to credit-bearing internships. Applicability to non-credit-bearing internships to be confirmed with CAO.

6. Events

Employers can view upcoming CAO-organised events (career fairs, roadshows, recruitment talks) from a dedicated events listing page. Table columns: event name, event type, date, status (Open, Registered, Closed). Each row links to an event detail page. Linear: SU-4482, SU-4361

6.2 Employer-Created Events

Employers can create their own on-campus or off-campus events. All employer-created events require CAO approval before appearing in the student portal.

Event creation form fields: Event name, event type, on-campus/off-campus toggle, description, up to 3 preferred date options, venue (on-campus: from CAO venue list; off-campus: free text), estimated attendance, logistics requirements. Employer saves as draft before submitting.

On-campus vs off-campus: On-campus events are flagged for venue booking review by CAO. Off-campus events are tagged as "Off-Campus" in the CAO admin view to indicate lighter vetting is required.

Employer event workflow status (visible to employer): Requested → Approved → Waiting for Requirements → Live/Published.

Post-approval: Approved events are published to the student portal. Employers cannot edit or cancel after approval — they must submit a withdrawal request to CAO. System sends automated reminders to employer to complete any missing information prior to the event. Employer can view registrant count and attendance (CAO-configurable visibility — on by default).

7. Internship Supervisor Access

Internship Supervisors are not platform account holders. When a student assessment is required, the system sends an automated email to the assigned Internship Supervisor with a unique, time-limited access link. Clicking the link takes the supervisor directly to the assessment form for that specific student — no login or account creation required. Once the form is submitted, the link expires. Supervisors have no persistent access to the platform.
